## About

This portal is a project of the **Canadian Census Data Discovery Partnership (CCDDP)**. The CCDDP project aims to facilitate historical research by reducing the barriers to access and use of Canadian census data. Using the search tab of this website you can discover historical and contemporary Canadian census materials held by numerous institutions across the country.

Censuses, or population counts, have been conducted in the territory now known as Canada since 1665-66 in New France. Canada’s census is our most valuable primary economic, social, and cultural data set, and is an essential research tool for the formation of new knowledge and understanding about the populations that lived here in the past and present.

The sources of information that make up these censuses are rich, diverse, and complex. They consist of databases of archival records, publications, and data files and types, containing a mix of primary government data, expert analyses, and supporting documentation. This project is focused on inventorying the sources that contain data and documentation that supports the use of census data and exploring mechanisms for improved discovery of census data in Canada.

## Census Portal & Inventory

This Census Portal allows you to search the CCDDP census inventory of historical and contemporary Canadian census data held by numerous institutions across the country.
 

## Inventory scope

The CCDDP census inventory covers data produced or generated for the population censuses going back to the first population counts conducted by colonial governments in the 17th and 18th centuries, and continues to the present day.
 

**Disclaimer:** The census inventory contents are still being populated into this proof-of-concept portal, but will eventually include up to the 2021 census. As of 2025, there are currently over 18,000 census items inventoried for discovery and access by researchers and the general public. 

Materials included in the inventory falls into the following categories:

#### Data products

- Aggregate data tables

- Microdata

- Data documentation

#### Spatial information

- Digital spatial data (e.g. GIS data)

    - Reference maps

        - Thematic maps

#### Census analysis

- Analytical reports

    - Monographs

#### Documentation

- Accompanying materials: user guides, manuals, questionnaires

    - General and item-specific reference materials

        - Technical reports

            - Geographic reference materials & attribute products

#### Archival materials

- Original census returns, when searchable access to digitized records is available

The inventory includes only data products that were released as part of a population census. The Census of Agriculture, and variables related to agriculture, industry, merchandising, trade or transportation, are out of scope in this project, as are additional post-censal surveys and intercensal estimates. While the inventory includes a lot of primary data sources, we have also included data products that were created or derived from original Census items and products at the time, improving the usefulness of census records for research (such as digital or digitized tables and datasets generated from scanned print publications such as geospatial boundary files digitized from print maps, and, survey microdata files created from archival census records).
 

In addition to these digital data files, the inventory includes all known documentation related to census materials, and often includes supplementary documentation such as guides, manuals, reference products, or metadata records created by third parties. More information about the project’s scope is available on the Vision and Scope page of the CCDDP website.

If you know of a data product or supporting documentation that is in scope but is not currently in the portal, please contact us at census@scholarsportal.info and let us know!

## Inventory structure

The inventory consists of a custom set of metadata fields described in the Data Dictionary (link). Each inventoried item has a parallel metadata record in both the English and the French inventories.
 

Some fields are populated from an authority list. The following authority lists have been created by the CCDDP project. These authority lists often contain additional contextual information, such as definitions and date ranges for which terminology was in use:

## Data discovery and access

In addition to searching the content in this proof-of-concept portal, you may download the full database (link to Borealis). The database has been released under a CC-BY license. 

Please note that the CCDDP portal is a metadata catalogue that provides links to the data custodian’s copy of the item. As such, links in the inventory will break from time to time. Please contact us at census@scholarsportal.info to report broken links or if you have any questions. 

The CCDDP project advocates for national collaboration to ensure long-term preservation and access to the wide variety of historical census data sources held by government and academic institutions. If you are a creator of secondary census data products, we encourage you to consider sharing your research outputs in a repository such as Borealis and to let us know about your work 

## Did you know?
 

If you cite or tag your datasets with keywords including source materials such as “census data”, we can include it in the CCDDP inventory and help others discover your work.

## Harmful language statement

Within the historical Census of Canada data collection, users will encounter offensive and harmful terminology and depictions of people and places, reflective of Canada’s colonial past. While it is important to accurately reflect the historical record in order to grapple with its legacy, the CCDDP project is committed to using respectful terminology in our metadata and discovery tools and to providing content warnings where appropriate. The project also aims to develop educational resources to support a nuanced understanding of the historical factors which shaped how census data were collected, described, and disseminated throughout Canada’s history.
 

If you can support us in these efforts, or see something harmful on our proof-of-concept discovery portal that we could correct, we encourage you to get in touch with us at census@scholarsportal.info.
